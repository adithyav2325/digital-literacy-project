**Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud via Online Classifieds**



What the crime is: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud occurs when cybercriminals manipulate victims into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive PINs under false pretenses.



How it happens (Step-by-step): > 1. A college student looking for a budget-friendly PC gaming controller finds an incredibly cheap listing on a local student marketplace or social media group.

2\. The "seller" (scammer) claims high demand and asks for a small token advance via UPI to lock in the deal.

3\. After the student sends the initial amount, the scammer claims the payment failed or requires a "merchant verification" step.

4\. The scammer sends a QR code or a payment request link, instructing the student to scan it and enter their UPI PIN to "receive a refund" or "authenticate the account."

5\. Unaware that entering a UPI PIN is only required to send money, not receive it, the student enters the PIN, authorizing a massive deduction from their bank account.



Who is usually targeted: College students and young adults are frequent targets because they often seek budget deals, second-hand electronics, and quick transactions.



Consequences: The immediate consequence is direct financial loss. Secondary consequences include severe stress, potential loss of funds needed for tuition or rent, and the hassle of freezing bank accounts to prevent further unauthorized access.
